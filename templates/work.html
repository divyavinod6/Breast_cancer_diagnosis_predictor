HOW THIS WORKS

We have used Naice Bayes classification to train our model.

Naive Bayes classification can be used to predict whether a given patient has malignant (cancerous) or benign (non-cancerous) breast tumors based on certain features or attributes.

The dataset is prepared, consisting of labeled examples of breast tumor data. Each example includes several features or attributes of the tumor, such as its size, shape, texture, and other characteristics, along with the corresponding label indicating whether it is malignant or benign.

The performance of the this Naive Bayes classifier is evaluated using splitting the dataset into training and testing sets and has aquired accuracy of 97.5%
